GRAPHITE PENCILS

HB:
Balanced pencil suitable for smooth writing and sketching.

2B:
Softer than HB and used for layering for the extra darkness.

4B:
Good for shading.

6B:
Dark and soft, Which is exactly used for the second layering to bring the realistic effect.

8B:
Darker than the 6B pencil and it may be used for darkening the outlining the portrait.

10B:
This is the pencil which has more broder lead and it is used for shading the darkest part like hairs,eyelids etc,...

CHARCOAL

Soft:
Soft pencils are used to show the light effects in the portrait.

Medium:
This is little bit darker than the Soft pencils.

Hard:
This pencil is the darkest in the charcoal pencils it will help to highlight the major difference in the portrait to show the major difference to show the realism in that drawing.

CHARCOAL

Charcoal creates rich dark marks.

GRAPHITE

Graphite creates smooth controlled lines.

CHARCOAL VS GRAPHITE

Charcoal:
Darker
More expressive

Graphite:
Cleaner
More controlled
